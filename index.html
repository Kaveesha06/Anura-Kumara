About Me

Born on November 24, 1968, Anura Kumara Dissanayake is a transformative leader dedicated to social justice and progressive change in Sri Lanka.

From his early days with the JVP to becoming the leader of the National People's Power (NPP), he has consistently fought for equitable development and challenged systemic injustices.

Political Journey

Early Activism

Engaged with JVP movements in the 1980s, demonstrating early commitment to social transformation.

Parliamentary Entry

Elected to parliament in 2000, serving as Commercial Agriculture Minister for Kurunegala District in 2004.

Presidential Victory

Historic election in September 2024 as the first third-party leader to become President of Sri Lanka.

Key Achievements

Economic Reform

Reduced current tax levels and eliminated VAT on essential goods, providing economic relief.

Land Reform

Redistributed 11,000 acres from Kantale Sugar Corporation to small entrepreneurs.

Judicial Review

Initiated review of over 400 cases and expedited investigations into major national incidents.
